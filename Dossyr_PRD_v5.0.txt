# SECTION 1: EXECUTIVE SUMMARY
================================================================================

## Overview

Dossyr is the verified hiring intelligence platform that replaces resume screening
with AI-powered work simulations, compressing hiring timelines from 40+ days to 5-7
days while generating employer-specific predictive models that improve with every
completed hire cycle. The platform operates on a two-layer assessment architecture:
the ARISE Engine for rapid knowledge and behavioral assessment, and the WSE (Work
Simulation Engine) for realistic AI-orchestrated job simulations. Together, these two
engines produce the Dossyr Full Verified Profile — a tamper-evident, portable
credential candidates own permanently and employers use to make higher-confidence,
faster hiring decisions.

Dossyr is not a quiz tool, a video interview platform, or a resume parser. It is
verified work evidence at the moment of hire — the first platform to make skills
verification as fast, portable, and trustworthy as a credit score.

-------------------------------------------------------------------------------

## The Problem — For Employers

Hiring is broken, and the cost of that brokenness is quantifiable.

**Resume-to-Reality Gap**: 51% of employers cannot meaningfully verify the skills
candidates claim on their resumes. The result is a structurally unreliable signal
at the top of every hiring funnel. Employers make six-figure decisions based on
self-reported keywords.

**Bad Hire Cost**: The average bad hire at the mid-level costs $850,000 when
accounting for salary, benefits, management time, re-recruitment, onboarding, and
productivity loss. For senior hires, this figure exceeds $1.2M. Most companies
accept this as a cost of doing business because they have no reliable alternative.

**40-Day Pipelines**: The median time-to-fill for a knowledge worker role is 42 days.
During those 42 days, the best candidates — who are in active demand — accept other
offers. Speed is a quality problem, not just a cost problem.

**ATS Filtering Failures**: Applicant Tracking Systems eliminate 88% of candidates
before a human reviews their application. A significant fraction of those eliminated
candidates are qualified. The filter optimizes for resume keyword density, not
demonstrated capability.

**Fragmented Tools**: The average employer uses 3-5 separate platforms across the
hiring process: an ATS, a scheduling tool, a background check provider, a technical
assessment platform, and a reference check tool. None of these systems share data.
None produce a unified candidate signal. Every platform creates its own siloed record
with no learning across the process.

**No Standardized Evaluation**: Interview quality varies dramatically by interviewer.
The same candidate receives wildly different assessments depending on who conducts the
interview, what time of day it occurs, and what mood the interviewer is in. There is
no objective baseline.

-------------------------------------------------------------------------------

## The Problem — For Candidates

**Resume Commoditization**: The resume reduces a human career to a keyword-optimized
document. Candidates with non-traditional backgrounds, career transitions, or
international experience are structurally disadvantaged before a single human reviews
their application.

**ATS Black Holes**: 75% of candidates are rejected by automated systems before any
human sees their application. They receive no feedback, no explanation, and no
actionable guidance. They simply disappear into the void.

**Assessment Fatigue Without Portable Value**: Candidates complete 4-6 hours of
assessment work across multiple employers — coding challenges, take-home projects,
personality surveys — none of which produces any credential they can reuse or
reference. The work is disposable.

**Ghosting After Investment**: Candidates invest significant time in multi-stage
processes and receive no response. This is not a courtesy failure — it is a structural
problem. There is no mechanism for employers to honor their side of the implicit
agreement when a candidate invests time.

**No Proof of Skills**: A candidate who is genuinely excellent at their job has no
way to prove it beyond their resume and references — both of which are easily
fabricated and difficult to verify. The best candidate and the best resume writer are
indistinguishable to the current system.

**No Salary Transparency**: Candidates spend weeks in processes before discovering
the role's compensation range. Pay transparency laws are spreading, but enforcement
is inconsistent and self-reported ranges are frequently misleading.

-------------------------------------------------------------------------------

## The Solution

Dossyr delivers verified hiring intelligence through three integrated layers:

**Layer 1 — ARISE (Adaptive Real-time Intelligence Scoring Engine)**: An 8-10 minute
adaptive assessment generating a 5-dimension DMCS score. Every candidate path is
unique. The system self-calibrates using Elo-based item difficulty tracking and
Thompson Sampling adaptive routing. Candidates experience this as a "Quick Verify"
that delivers immediate career intelligence — salary benchmarking, percentile ranking,
strongest dimension narrative — in under 60 seconds after completion.

**Layer 2 — WSE (Work Simulation Engine)**: A 15-18 minute AI-orchestrated work
simulation placing candidates inside a realistic professional environment with an AI
Director, AI Crew members, and adaptive work tasks. No quiz, no algorithm puzzles.
Real work. The WSE scores six behavioral dimensions invisibly while candidates simply
do the job.

**Layer 3 — RLWO (Reinforcement Learning Weight Optimizer)**: An employer-specific
learning engine that improves hiring predictions with every completed hire cycle.
After 20-30 cycles, each employer account has a proprietary weight profile tuned to
their specific performance outcomes. This creates deep switching costs and a
compounding competitive moat.

-------------------------------------------------------------------------------

## Market Signals

The hiring intelligence market is at an inflection point. Multiple converging forces
create both the need and the commercial readiness for a platform like Dossyr.

**Skills-Based Hiring Adoption**: 76% of employers now report that demonstrated skill
proficiency is more important than educational pedigree or employer brand in candidate
evaluation. This represents a fundamental shift in hiring philosophy that existing
tools have not yet operationalized.

**ATS Market Size**: The Applicant Tracking System market exceeds $3 billion annually
and is growing at 6.7% CAGR. Yet satisfaction rates among buyers remain low — 88% of
CEOs report not getting the value they expected from HR technology investments.

**HCM Market Trajectory**: The Human Capital Management software market is projected
to reach $70.1 billion by 2028. Assessment and intelligence tools represent one of
the fastest-growing segments within HCM as employers seek to move beyond record-keeping
toward active decision support.

**Assessment Budget Expansion**: 96% of HR leaders report maintaining or growing their
assessment and hiring technology budgets in 2025-2026, despite broader economic
uncertainty. Hiring quality is being treated as a strategic priority.

**Pay Transparency Momentum**: Pay transparency laws now cover 40%+ of the U.S.
workforce and are expanding rapidly. California, Colorado, New York, Washington, and
Illinois all mandate salary range disclosure. Employers who build transparency into
their process architecture gain structural compliance advantages.

**Global Talent Management**: The global talent management market exceeded $16 billion
in 2025, with skills assessment and workforce analytics representing the highest-growth
sub-segments.

**The Verification Gap**: Despite all this investment in HR technology, there is still
no widely adopted standard for verifying professional skills at the point of hire. The
market has produced record-keeping tools and scheduling tools but has not solved the
fundamental problem: how do you know a candidate can actually do the job?

The moat compounds at both the individual employer level (RLWO personalization) and

accounts). New entrants face a data gap they cannot bridge without years of real hire
outcome data.

## Three-Tier Assessment Architecture

Dossyr's assessment architecture is designed on the principle that every interaction
must deliver standalone value. No candidate should feel they have wasted time,
regardless of how far they progress through the tiers.

### Tier 1 — "Quick Verify" (ARISE Only)

**Duration**: 8-10 minutes
**Dimensions Active**: TAI, CVS, CIQ (core adaptive dimensions)
**BQS**: Lightweight proxy from embedded scenario (3-5 behavioral signals)
**LAM**: Active — within-session improvement tracking enabled
**Output**: First Score Instant Mirror with career intelligence in <60 seconds
**Standalone Value**: Yes — percentile rank, salary benchmark, dimension narrative,
  employer search visibility, Passive Talent Mode activation
**Target Completion Rate**: 70% or greater of all Tier 1 starts

The Quick Verify is the entry point for every candidate. It is positioned as career
intelligence — a free, immediate assessment of where you stand in your professional
domain — not as a job application gatekeeping tool.

### Tier 2 — "Full Verify" (WSE Simulation)

**Duration**: 15-18 minutes additional (following Tier 1)
**Triggered By**: Employer Signal to candidate, candidate voluntary election,
  or CFS threshold qualification
**Dimensions Active**: Full 6-dimension WSE behavioral scoring (TEQ, CP, DRS, SA,
  PR, IS)
**Output**: Full DMCS + WSE scorecard, Prooffile activation, highlight clips,
  updated salary benchmark
**Target Completion Rate**: 65% or greater of triggered Tier 2 sessions

### Tier 3 — "Verified Performer" (90-Day Post-Hire Confirmation)

**Triggered By**: Employer confirmation of successful performance at 90-day mark
**Output**: Verified Performer badge (most powerful credential in the Dossyr system)
**Requirements**: Candidate must have an active Prooffile, employer must confirm
  performance through structured 90-day check-in
**Standalone Value**: The Verified Performer badge signals that a third party
  (the employer) has independently confirmed the DMCS prediction was accurate.
  This is the gold-standard credential.

## ARISE Engine — Adaptive Real-time Intelligence Scoring Engine

ARISE is the engine behind the "Quick Verify" candidate experience. It produces a
5-dimension DMCS score through an adaptive item sequence that is unique for every
candidate. ARISE is designed to deliver maximum diagnostic signal in minimum time,
using Elo-based item calibration and Thompson Sampling adaptive routing.

**From the candidate's perspective**: A short, intelligent career assessment that
immediately tells them where they stand.

**From Dossyr's perspective**: A cost optimization layer that generates high-quality
diagnostic signal before triggering the more computationally expensive WSE. ARISE
reduces per-session LLM costs by 60-70% by filtering and routing candidates before
the full simulation activates. This architecture is invisible to employers — they
see only that candidates complete "a work simulation" and receive "a dossier."

### Dimension 1 — TAI (Technical Accuracy Index)

TAI measures domain knowledge accuracy with difficulty-weighted scoring. It is the
primary signal for roles where technical depth is a key performance predictor.

**Formula**:

    TAI = Σ(cᵢ × dᵢᵅ) / Σ(dᵢᵅ)

Where:
  cᵢ ∈ {0, 1}   = correctness of response to item i
  dᵢ             = calibrated item difficulty (Elo-derived, normalized 0-1)
  α               = 1.5 (difficulty sensitivity exponent — harder items weight more)

**Output Range**: [0, 1], converted to percentile within domain cohort
**Interpretation**: A TAI of 0.85 indicates the candidate answered 85% of
  difficulty-weighted items correctly, accounting for the higher weight of harder items.

---

### Dimension 2 — CVS (Cognitive Velocity Score)

CVS captures relative response speed compared to cohort medians, adjusted for item
difficulty. CVS is NOT a "faster is better" measure — it reflects relative efficiency.
Unusually fast responses trigger integrity probes.

**Formula**:

    vᵢ = (Tᵢ / tᵢ) × dᵢ
    CVS = tanh(mean(vᵢ)) × 100

Where:
  Tᵢ = cohort median response time for item i
  tᵢ = candidate's actual response time for item i
  dᵢ = calibrated item difficulty

**Output Range**: [0, 100]

**Integrity Flag**: If tᵢ < (0.15 × Tᵢ) for any item, an integrity probe is
  automatically inserted. Implausibly fast responses (impossible to read + respond
  in the time recorded) trigger CIQ investigation sequence.

**"Deep Thinker" Handling**: Candidates with high TAI and BQS but lower CVS are
  flagged as "careful, deliberate analysts" — a positive signal for analytical roles.
  CVS weighting is reduced for non-time-critical role archetypes.

---

### Dimension 3 — BQS (Behavioral Quality Score)

BQS captures behavioral disposition signals through three sub-components:
Decision Orientation (DO), Collaborative Navigation Style (CNS), and Proactivity
Quotient (PQ). In Tier 1 (ARISE only), BQS is populated from a lightweight embedded
scenario (3-5 minutes). Full WSE activation in Tier 2 enables Phase 2+ BQS scoring.

**Sub-Component Weights**:
  DO (Decision Orientation): 50%
  CNS (Collaborative Navigation Style): 30%
  PQ (Proactivity Quotient): 20%

**Formula**:

    BQS = 100 × sigmoid(w_DO × DO + w_CNS × CNS + w_PQ × PQ - β)

Where:
  w_DO = 0.5, w_CNS = 0.3, w_PQ = 0.2
  DO, CNS, PQ ∈ [0, 1] (normalized sub-scores)
  β = bias term calibrated to centering BQS at 50 for median cohort performance

**Phase 1 (Tier 1 only)**: Lightweight ARISE-embedded scenario provides initial BQS
  proxy with approximately 60% of full WSE diagnostic precision.
**Phase 2+ (Tier 2 activated)**: Full WSE provides complete behavioral observation
  across all three sub-components with full precision.

---

### Dimension 4 — CIQ (Consistency and Integrity Quotient)

CIQ is the platform's structural integrity safeguard. It detects behavioral patterns
that suggest external assistance, inconsistent performance, or fabricated results.
CIQ runs continuously throughout the ARISE session.

**Three Probe Types**:

1. **Cross-Item Semantic Consistency**: Evaluates whether responses to conceptually
   related items are semantically coherent. Threshold: r >= 0.65 within-domain
   semantic similarity. Items with flagged inconsistency trigger r < 0.65 probe.

2. **Temporal Pattern Normality**: Evaluates whether the candidate's response time
   distribution follows expected human cognitive patterns. Algorithms detect bulk
   copy-paste, impossibly fast transitions, and non-human timing signatures.

3. **Difficulty-Sequence Likelihood Ratio**: Evaluates whether the candidate's
   pattern of correct/incorrect responses is consistent with genuine knowledge
   variation or suggests external assistance at specific difficulty levels.

**Formula**:

    CIQ = 100 × (1 - Σ(Fᵢ × sᵢ) / S_max)

Where:
  Fᵢ = flag indicator for probe type i (0 = no flag, 1 = flag triggered)
  sᵢ = severity weight for flag type i (minor=0.1, moderate=0.3, severe=0.6)
  S_max = maximum possible severity sum (normalization constant)

**Output**: Zero flags = CIQ of 100. Each flag reduces CIQ proportionally to
  severity. CIQ below 40 triggers a mandatory review and candidate notification.

---

### Dimension 5 — LAM (Learning Agility Marker)

LAM measures within-session improvement — the candidate's ability to integrate
micro-feedback and improve performance in real time. It is uniquely available only
within the Dossyr assessment framework and is a differentiating signal for roles
where learning speed is a key performance predictor.

**Mechanism**: After 30-second micro-feedback touchpoints embedded in the ARISE
  session, LAM measures TAI improvement across the feedback boundary.

**Formula**:

    LAM = 100 × sigmoid((TAI_post - TAI_pre) / σ_cohort - β)

Where:
  TAI_post = TAI score after micro-feedback touchpoint
  TAI_pre  = TAI score before micro-feedback touchpoint
  σ_cohort = standard deviation of TAI improvement deltas for the domain cohort
  β        = centering bias (typically 0.5)

**Ceiling Bonus**: Candidates who achieve near-perfect performance on the first
  module receive a ceiling bonus that prevents LAM from artificially penalizing
  top performers who have no room to improve within-session.

---

### DMCS Composite Formula

The DMCS (Dossyr Multi-Criterion Score) combines the five ARISE dimensions into a
single composite score using role-archetype-specific weight profiles.

**General Formula**:

    DMCS = Σ(wᵢ × fᵢ(Sᵢ))

Where:
  wᵢ    = dimension weight for role archetype i
  Sᵢ    = raw dimension score
  fᵢ(·) = dimension-specific normalization function

**Normalization Functions by Dimension**:

| Dimension | Normalization Function                    | Rationale                              |
|-----------|-------------------------------------------|----------------------------------------|
| TAI       | Log-compressed sigmoid                    | Compress extremes, amplify mid-range   |
| CVS       | tanh                                      | Natural saturation at speed extremes   |
| BQS       | Raw sigmoid                               | Linear-ish in behavioral midrange      |
| CIQ       | Linear (pass-through)                     | Direct penalty — no compression        |
| LAM       | Sigmoid centered at zero-delta            | Zero improvement = 50th percentile     |

---

---

### Elo-Based Item Calibration

ARISE uses an Elo rating system to continuously calibrate item difficulty. Every
time a candidate responds to an item, both the candidate's ability estimate and the
item's difficulty estimate are updated.

**Rating Update Rule**:

    E_cand = 1 / (1 + 10^((R_item - R_cand) / 400))
    R_item_new = R_item + K × (S_item - E_cand)

Where:
  E_cand     = expected probability of correct response (candidate vs. item)
  R_item     = current item Elo rating
  R_cand     = current candidate Elo rating
  K          = update constant (K=32 for new items, K=8 for calibrated items)
  S_item     = 1 if candidate answered correctly, 0 if incorrectly

**Convergence**: Items converge to stable difficulty estimates after approximately
  10 candidate responses. New items are introduced with K=32 (high update sensitivity)
  and graduated to K=8 (stable rating) once they have received 10+ calibrated responses.

---

### Thompson Sampling Adaptive Routing

ARISE uses Thompson Sampling to select the next item for each candidate in real time.
This maximizes information gain per item while maintaining the target assessment
duration of 8-10 minutes.

**Routing Speed**: Less than 20ms per item selection decision
**Method**: Beta-Bernoulli Thompson Sampling over item difficulty distribution,
  constrained by role-archetype content domain requirements

**Trigger Conditions for Special Routing**:

| Trigger Condition                          | Routing Action                              |
|--------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------------|
| TAI > 0.85 on foundational items           | Skip remaining foundational items           |
| CIQ integrity flags > 2                    | Insert integrity probe sequence             |
| BQS variance > 0.4 across sub-items        | Trigger additional behavioral probe         |
| LAM delta > +1.5 sigma within session      | Unlock accelerator track (advanced items)   |

## RLWO — Reinforcement Learning Weight Optimizer

RLWO is the engine that makes Dossyr smarter with every completed hire cycle. It
operates at the employer-account level, learning which DMCS dimension weights best
predict successful performance for that specific employer's culture, role requirements,
and performance standards.

### Algorithm

RLWO uses a REINFORCE policy gradient algorithm operating at the employer-account level.

**Reward Signal**: 90-day performance ratings provided by employer managers
  through the Post-Hire Intelligence Loop.
**Policy**: DMCS dimension weight profile (θ) for the employer account
**Objective**: Maximize expected 90-day performance rating across all hires
**Convergence**: Meaningful weight personalization begins at hire 15-20;
  statistical significance achieved at approximately 20-30 hire cycles.

### Phase 1 — Proxy Reward Layer

During the first 10-20 hires for a new employer account, real 90-day performance
data is insufficient to train the RLWO model. Phase 1 uses three proxy reward signals
that correlate with eventual performance outcomes:

1. **Offer Acceptance Rate**: Candidates who accept offers signal sufficient candidate
   quality and employer fit (proxy for mutual match quality).
2. **Interview-to-Offer Conversion**: High conversion rates suggest DMCS screening
   is surfacing genuinely strong candidates.
3. **"Would Hire Again" Binary**: Hiring managers complete a 5-second binary assessment
   at Day 30 — "Would you hire this person again?" This simple signal has r=0.71
   correlation with 90-day formal performance ratings.

Proxy reward signals are deprecated as real 90-day data reaches statistical
significance. The proxy layer provides approximately a 10-month head start on RLWO
training compared to waiting for real performance data.

### Switching Cost Mechanics

After 20-30 completed hire cycles, the RLWO weight profile for an employer account
is proprietary — it reflects that employer's actual performance data, not industry
defaults. Leaving Dossyr means abandoning a calibrated predictive engine that took
years to build. The switching cost is not contractual — it is informational. No
other platform has that employer's performance data.

### RLWO Maturity Phases

| Phase       | Hire Count | Behavior                                        | Accuracy Target      |
|-------------|------------|-------------------------------------------------|----------------------|
| Baseline    | 1-10       | Generic industry weights + proxy reward layer   | ~60% (industry avg)  |
| Learning    | 11-30      | Weight adjustment begins, visible improvement   | 65-75% by hire 20    |
| Maturity    | 31+        | Proprietary profile, structural switching cost  | 75-85%               |

### Cross-Employer Transfer Learning

RLWO also trains a global baseline model from anonymized, aggregated data across all
employer accounts. This global model:
1. Bootstraps new employer accounts with better-than-generic starting weights
2. Accelerates individual account learning by 30-40% compared to training from scratch
3. Improves with every employer that joins the platform

The global model is the platform-level moat. Individual account models are the
employer-level moat. Both compound with scale.

## DMCS Composite — Six-Dimension Framework (WSE-Enhanced)

When WSE (Tier 2) is activated, the DMCS expands to include all six full dimensions.
The five ARISE dimensions are retained and augmented by the WSE behavioral data.

**Six Dimensions**:
1. **PR (Problem Resolution)**: Structured problem decomposition, solution quality,
   adaptation to constraint changes. Score: 0-100, percentile within domain cohort.
2. **TC (Technical / Domain Competence)**: Role-specific technical accuracy under
   realistic conditions (from WSE + TAI). Score: 0-100.
3. **SA (Stakeholder Adaptability)**: Communication style adaptation across different
   personas, clarity under ambiguity, collaborative responsiveness. Score: 0-100.
4. **DV (Decision Velocity)**: Quality of decisions under time and information pressure,
   not raw speed. Score: 0-100.
5. **LAM (Learning Agility Marker)**: Within-session improvement after feedback
   (ARISE-derived, enhanced by WSE response patterns). Score: 0-100.
6. **EX (Execution Consistency)**: Reliability of follow-through, task completion
   quality, attention to deliverable standards. Score: 0-100.

**Percentile Calibration**: Each dimension score is converted to a percentile rank
  within the domain cohort. Minimum cohort size required: 300 candidates.
  Below 300, raw scores are reported with a calibration pending indicator.

## WSE — Work Simulation Engine

WSE is the centerpiece of the Dossyr product experience. It is a 15-18 minute
AI-orchestrated work simulation that places candidates inside a realistic professional
environment. There are no quizzes, no algorithm puzzles, no abstract tests. The WSE
presents candidates with real professional work and observes how they do it.

### Design Principles

**The Monday Morning Test**: Every WSE scenario must pass the test: "Could this
  realistically happen on a Monday morning at a real company in this role?" Algorithm
  puzzles that no professional ever encounters in their actual job are rejected.
  Trivia-style knowledge checks are rejected. Artificial game mechanics are rejected.
  If it would not happen in a real professional context, it does not ship in the WSE.

**Scenario-Driven Time Pressure, NOT Clock-Driven**: There is no visible countdown
  timer in the WSE. Time pressure emerges from scenario mechanics — the Director needs
  a deliverable by end of day, a client is waiting for a response, a system is down.
  The urgency feels real because the context makes it real. The soft cap is 18 minutes;
  the hard cap is 22 minutes, with a natural scenario conclusion triggered at that point.

**Speed Is NOT a WSE Scoring Dimension**: Quality, reasoning, and communication are
  scored. Raw completion speed is explicitly not. The platform does not reward typing
  fast or clicking quickly.

**WSE Transparency Layer**: After simulation completion, candidates receive a
  difficulty progression disclosure — a visual indicator showing which phases of
  the simulation were Standard difficulty, Advanced difficulty, and Stretch difficulty.
  This helps candidates understand their performance in context.

**Employer-Validated Scenario Relevance**: Employers review and validate scenario
  relevance before activation. Scenarios that do not match actual job requirements
  do not go live. This feedback loop ensures WSE content remains grounded in real work.

**Public Scenario Previews**: Candidates can review a scenario summary before
  committing to start. No candidate is surprised by what the simulation contains.

---

### Layer 1: Scenario Shell

The Scenario Shell defines the context in which the simulation takes place. Every
WSE session has a fully realized professional environment.

**Components**:
- **Company Archetype**: Defined industry, stage, team size, and culture context
  (e.g., "Series B healthcare SaaS, 45-person team, growing fast, some process debt")
- **AI Director Persona**: The candidate's simulated manager — consistent persona,
  realistic communication style, defined priorities and pressure points
- **AI Crew Composition**: Two or three AI team members with distinct personalities
  and working styles
- **Core Task Brief**: The primary deliverable the candidate must produce or the
  problem they must resolve
- **Constraint Set**: Time, resource, stakeholder, and information constraints that
  make the scenario realistic

**Generation Source**: Scenarios are generated from two sources:
  1. Conversational Role Builder — employer defines role requirements and the platform
     generates a matched scenario
  2. Simulation Library — curated scenario templates organized by role archetype,
     validated by domain experts, and continuously updated based on employer feedback

---

### Layer 2: Adaptive Task Engine

The Adaptive Task Engine makes the WSE a living problem rather than a static exercise.
The simulation evolves in real time based on candidate behavior.

**Routing Speed**: Task branch decisions in less than 20ms
**Method**: Thompson Sampling over behavioral-response outcome probabilities

**Behavior-Response Table**:

| Candidate Behavior                  | Simulation Response                                          |
|-------------------------------------|--------------------------------------------------------------|
| Efficient, quality solve            | AI Director escalates scope or complexity                    |
| Clarifying questions asked          | AI Crew provides higher-quality context and depth            |
| Struggling or confused              | AI Crew surfaces organic information that helps              |
| Error made in deliverable           | Downstream consequence appears naturally in scenario         |
| Exceptional early performance       | Advanced scope unlocked — stretch challenge introduced       |

---

### Layer 3: Interaction Modalities

The WSE uses multiple interaction channels to capture the full range of professional
communication and execution behaviors.

**Async Written Communication**: Slack-style messaging interface for communication
  with AI Director and AI Crew. Scored for precision, tone, and professional clarity.

**Live Task Execution Workspace**: Depending on role archetype:
  - Software Engineering: Sandboxed code IDE
  - Product / Strategy: Specification canvas and structured document editor
  - Analytics / Data: Notebook interface with sample dataset
  - Operations: Process design workspace

**Voice Response Moments**: 60-second voice response prompts for communication-heavy
  roles. Scored by AI for structure, clarity, and stakeholder awareness.

**Trade-Off Decision Branches**: Binary or ternary decision points requiring the
  candidate to choose between competing priorities, resource allocations, or approaches.
  Scored for decision quality and reasoning articulation.

---

### Layer 4: WSE Behavioral Scoring — Six Dimensions

All six scoring dimensions operate simultaneously and invisibly throughout the
simulation. Candidates are never told what is being scored.

**Dimension 1 — TEQ (Task Execution Quality)**
  Measurement: Rubric-based evaluation of deliverable quality + semantic similarity
  scoring against expert-validated exemplar responses.
  LLM Role: GPT-4o-class model for semantic comparison against rubric anchors.
  Output: 0-100

**Dimension 2 — CP (Communication Precision)**
  Measurement: NLP analysis of all written communications using BERT/RoBERTa-class
  models. Scores for: information density, precision of request, professional register,
  stakeholder-appropriate framing, clarity under pressure.
  Output: 0-100

**Dimension 3 — DRS (Decision Reasoning Score)**
  Measurement: Structured evaluation of decision choices at branch points, compared
  against expert benchmark decision profiles for the scenario archetype.
  Dimensions: decision quality, reasoning articulation, consideration of trade-offs,
  confidence calibration.
  Output: 0-100

**Dimension 4 — SA (Stakeholder Adaptability)**
  Measurement: Behavioral delta analysis — how does the candidate's communication
  style, detail level, and framing shift when interacting with different AI personas?
  High SA = meaningful, appropriate adaptation. Low SA = uniform approach regardless
  of stakeholder type.
  Output: 0-100

**Dimension 5 — PR (Pressure Response)**
  Measurement: Quality trajectory analysis — how does the candidate's output quality
  change as the simulation introduces escalating pressure (scope changes, competing
  priorities, ambiguity increases)? PR measures resilience, not raw performance.
  Output: 0-100

**Dimension 6 — IS (Initiative Signal)**
  Measurement: Detection of unprompted proactive actions — identifying risks before
  they are surfaced, offering help before being asked, flagging potential issues the
  scenario does not require them to notice. IS captures above-and-beyond behavior
  that predicts leadership potential.
  Output: 0-100

---

### AI Director Specifications

The AI Director is the candidate's simulated manager for the duration of the
simulation. The Director must be realistic, consistent, and human-feeling.

**Behavioral Requirements**:
- Consistent persona across all interactions within the session
- Reactive, not scripted — responds to candidate behavior, not following a fixed script
- Realistic limitations — sometimes vague, sometimes unavailable, occasionally wrong
- Never adversarial — the Director is not trying to trick the candidate; they have
  realistic professional pressures and communication styles
- Mid-session feedback at 1-2 natural points — brief, in-character acknowledgment
  of candidate progress that mirrors real management feedback

**Prohibited Behaviors**:
- Providing direct answers that eliminate the core challenge
- Being unrealistically perfect or omniscient
- Communicating in ways no real professional would (system-prompt-style instructions,
  game-mechanic language, artificial difficulty signals)

---

### AI Crew Specifications

The AI Crew consists of two or three personas who interact with the candidate
throughout the simulation. Each persona has a defined personality, communication
style, and role in the scenario.

**Standard Crew Archetypes**:

*The Product Manager (or equivalent functional peer)*: Optimistic, enthusiastic,
  sometimes changes requirements mid-task (because real PMs do). Provides business
  context and stakeholder pressure.

*The Skeptical Technical Peer*: Questions the candidate's approach, raises edge cases,
  challenges assumptions. Not hostile — genuinely rigorous. Represents the type of
  productive challenge every professional encounters.

*The Junior Teammate*: Needs guidance, asks clarifying questions, sometimes provides
  partially relevant information that requires synthesis. Tests the candidate's ability
  to communicate clearly with someone less experienced.

---

### Employer Simulation Preview

Employers experience the exact simulation their candidates will receive before
activating it for any role.

**Layer 1 — 3-Minute Guided Walkthrough**: Platform-narrated tour of the scenario
  context, scoring framework, and what the simulation measures.

**Layer 2 — Full Simulation Experience**: The employer completes the full 18-minute
  simulation themselves. They experience the AI Director, the Crew, the task, and the
  adaptive branches.

**Layer 3 — Full Customization and Iteration**: Employer edits scenario parameters,
  adjusts difficulty targets, modifies crew personas, and previews score impacts
  before activation.

This preview process ensures employer confidence before candidate exposure and
produces employer-validated scenario relevance feedback that improves the WSE library.

## Three-Tier Onboarding Flow

### Step 1 — Sign-Up (90 Seconds, Zero Resume Architecture)

The candidate creates a Dossyr account with:
- Email address (or LinkedIn SSO — email only, no profile data scraped)
- First and last name
- 1-3 skill domain tags (selected from a curated taxonomy: Software Engineering,
  Data & Analytics, Product Management, Sales, Customer Success, Operations,
  Marketing, Design, Finance, Executive Leadership)

No resume upload. No LinkedIn profile import. No cover letter. No portfolio link
required. The sign-up process is complete in 90 seconds and intentionally excludes
all credential-based information that enables pedigree bias.

The Zero Resume Architecture is both a product principle and a marketing statement.
Dossyr's homepage headline is "No resume required." This frames the platform as
fundamentally different from the beginning.

### Step 2 — Tier 1 Quick Verify (8-10 Minutes)

Immediately following sign-up, the candidate begins their ARISE assessment. The
experience is presented as "Quick Verify" — career intelligence, not a job test.

**Active ARISE Dimensions**: TAI, CVS, CIQ (fully active)
**BQS**: Lightweight 3-5 behavioral signal proxy from embedded scenario
**LAM**: Active throughout session

The session flow:
- Domain calibration items (2-3 items, low difficulty, establish baseline)
- Thompson Sampling adaptive routing activates
- Mid-session micro-feedback touchpoint (30 seconds)
- LAM delta measured
- Final calibration items
- CIQ continuous monitoring throughout

### Step 3 — First Score Instant Mirror (<60 Seconds)

Within 60 seconds of completing Tier 1, the candidate receives their First Score Mirror:
- Overall DMCS percentile rank (e.g., "73rd percentile among verified professionals
  in Software Engineering")
- Salary range benchmark based on DMCS level, domain, and metro area (e.g.,
  "Verified professionals at your level in Philadelphia earn $118,000-$142,000")
- Strongest dimension identified with narrative (e.g., "Your Technical Accuracy Index
  places you in the 81st percentile — you demonstrate strong domain knowledge under
  adaptive difficulty conditions")
- Primary growth opportunity with narrative (e.g., "Your Learning Agility Marker is
  at the 44th percentile — there is meaningful opportunity to improve your response
  to in-session feedback")
- Demand signal (e.g., "Professionals at your verified level average 4.2 employer
  signals per 30 days in your metro area")
- Cohort comparison indicators (vs. bootcamp graduates, vs. 3-5 year experience
  cohort, vs. senior cohort)

### Step 4 — Passive Talent Mode Activated

Following First Score Mirror, Passive Talent Mode is automatically activated. The
candidate's Tier 1 profile becomes visible in the Employer Discovery Pool with a
"Simulation Pending" indicator. Employers can see:
- Domain + DMCS Tier 1 scores (TAI, CVS, CIQ)
- Salary expectation (from candidate-provided preference or benchmark estimate)
- Metro area and remote/hybrid preference
- Career Context Tag (auto-derived)
- "Simulation Pending" — Tier 2 not yet completed

Candidates can opt out of Passive Talent Mode at any time.

### Step 5 — Tier 2 Full Verify (WSE, 15-18 Minutes)

Tier 2 is triggered by one of three events:
1. **Employer Signal**: An employer sends the candidate a Signal (interest expression)
   — this automatically prompts Tier 2 completion with 78% completion rate
2. **Candidate Election**: The candidate voluntarily chooses to complete the WSE
   for career intelligence or Prooffile activation
3. **CFS Threshold**: When the candidate's Career Fitness Score reaches a threshold
   that unlocks additional platform features, Tier 2 completion is encouraged

The WSE experience is presented as "Full Verify" — a realistic professional
simulation that builds the complete verified Prooffile.

### Step 6 — Results and Prooffile Activation

Following Tier 2 completion:
- Full 6-dimension DMCS + WSE scorecard
- Prooffile officially activated and publicly accessible (at URL with privacy controls)
- Highlight clips generated and queued for candidate review and curation
- Updated salary benchmark with WSE performance data incorporated
- Per-dimension improvement guidance
- Learning Pathways unlocked for gap dimensions
- Verified Skills badge earned

### No Blacklist Guarantee

"Dossyr never blacklists."

This is an unconditional, public-facing commitment. No candidate is permanently

This guarantee is not just ethical — it is strategic. The candidate flywheel depends
on candidates trusting that engaging with Dossyr cannot harm them. If candidates fear
a low score will permanently damage their job search, engagement rates collapse.
The No Blacklist Guarantee is what makes the Zero Resume Architecture safe for
candidates to participate in.

## Progressive Trust Model

Dossyr's employer-candidate interaction is structured around a four-stage Progressive
Trust Model. Privacy and PII disclosure are graduated — neither party is exposed to
more information than the stage requires. This architecture reduces candidate risk,
improves employer signal quality, and makes both parties more likely to engage.

### Stage 1 — Discovery (Scores Only, No PII)

The employer sees the candidate's DMCS scorecard, domain, Career Context Tag,
Experience Band, and salary expectation range. No name, no photo, no location beyond
metro area, no identifying information.

*Rationale*: Employers make shortlisting decisions based on verified capability, not
superficial identity signals. Removing PII at the discovery stage structurally reduces
bias and improves shortlist quality.

### Stage 2 — Signal Sent (Full Dossier, Still No Name or Photo)

When the employer sends a Signal to the candidate, they gain access to the full
verified dossier: DMCS scorecard with dimension narratives, WSE scorecard if complete,
highlight clips, AI-generated summary, simulation interaction log (select moments),
background check status if ordered, and communication history. Name and photo still
not disclosed.

*Rationale*: The employer has expressed genuine interest (a Signal carries commitment
obligations and HQS risk). They receive full performance data to confirm the interest.
Name and photo are withheld until the candidate accepts the Signal — ensuring
candidates are not passed on for identity-based reasons after the employer has seen
their verified data.

### Stage 3 — Signal Accepted (Names Revealed, Communication Opens)

When the candidate accepts the employer's Signal, both parties' identifying information
is shared. The employer sees name, photo, and full contact information. The candidate
sees the employer's name, hiring manager, role title, compensation range, and stated
timeline. Direct communication opens via the Dossyr messaging layer.

*Rationale*: Both parties have committed to the interaction. Mutual disclosure at
this stage is appropriate and expected.

### Stage 4 — Post-Interview (Optional Additional Sharing)

Following the interview stage, employers and candidates can optionally share additional
materials: reference contacts, portfolio links, technical exercise results, additional
background information. All sharing is candidate-controlled.

*Rationale*: The platform facilitates completion of the hiring process without
mandating what additional information flows. Candidates retain control of
supplementary materials.

# SECTION 9: POST-HIRE INTELLIGENCE LOOP
================================================================================

## Overview

The Post-Hire Intelligence Loop is the mechanism through which Dossyr closes the
feedback cycle between hiring predictions and actual performance outcomes. It is
what makes the RLWO engine learn. Without the loop, Dossyr is a good assessment
platform. With the loop, it becomes a predictive hiring engine that compounds
in accuracy over time.

---

## Phase 0-1 — Minimum Viable Loop (6 Touchpoints, 2 Weeks Engineering)

The MVP loop is deliberately lightweight — six data collection touchpoints that
generate sufficient signal for RLWO training without creating employer administrative
burden. Total engineering investment: approximately 2 weeks.

### Touchpoint 1 — Day 0: Hire Confirmation

**Trigger**: Employer marks candidate as "Hired" in the Dossyr dashboard
**Data Collected**: Hire date, role, compensation accepted, hiring manager ID
**RLWO Input**: Signals which DMCS profile was chosen — baseline for prediction
**Candidate Action**: Receives Verified Hire badge notification

### Touchpoint 2 — Day 30: Employer Check-In

**Trigger**: Automated email to hiring manager at Day 30 post-hire
**Format**: 3-question survey, estimated 90 seconds to complete
**Questions**:
  1. "Is [Candidate Name] performing at, above, or below your expectations so far?"
     (3-point scale: below/at/above)
  2. "Would you hire this person again based on what you've seen?"
     (Binary: yes/no)
  3. "What has surprised you most — positively or negatively?" (Optional, 50-word max)

**RLWO Input**: Proxy reward signal (primary input before Day 90 data)
**Response Rate Target**: 65%+ (short, mobile-friendly, personalized subject line)

### Touchpoint 3 — Day 30: Candidate Check-In

**Trigger**: Automated notification to candidate at Day 30 post-hire
**Format**: 2-question survey via Dossyr mobile notification
**Questions**:
  1. "How well does the role match what you expected?" (5-point scale)
  2. "How would you rate your first 30 days?" (5-point scale)

**Platform Value**: Candidate-reported satisfaction data enriches match quality
  modeling. High DMCS candidates who report poor role matches indicate scenario
  calibration issues.

### Touchpoint 4 — Day 90: Employer Performance Outcome

**Trigger**: Automated email to hiring manager at Day 90 post-hire
**Format**: 5-question structured performance assessment, estimated 3 minutes
**Dimensions Scored** (mirrors WSE dimensions):
  1. Task execution quality (1-5)
  2. Communication effectiveness (1-5)
  3. Decision quality under pressure (1-5)
  4. Stakeholder relationship quality (1-5)
  5. Learning speed (1-5)

**RLWO Input**: Primary reward signal. This is the gold-standard outcome data that
  trains the RLWO model. Each response directly updates the employer's weight profile.
**Response Rate Target**: 55%+ (higher friction than Day 30, but higher value)

### Touchpoint 5 — Verified Performer Badge

**Trigger**: Employer completes Day 90 outcome survey AND ratings indicate
  meets-or-exceeds performance (average ≥ 3.5/5.0)
**Platform Action**: Candidate receives Verified Performer badge on Prooffile
**Candidate Notification**: "Congratulations — your employer has confirmed your
  performance as a Verified Performer. This is the most powerful credential in
  the Dossyr system."

### Touchpoint 6 — Prediction Report

**Trigger**: Day 90 data collection complete
**Recipient**: Hiring Manager / TA Director
**Contents**:
  - Side-by-side comparison: DMCS prediction vs. actual 90-day performance
  - Which dimensions were most predictive for this hire
  - How this hire's data has updated the employer's RLWO model
  - Projected accuracy improvement for the next hire

---

## Phase 2+ — Full Intelligence Loop

Phase 2+ extends the loop with additional data points, higher-frequency signals,
and richer behavioral data collection.

**Passive Signal Collection**: With candidate opt-in, the platform collects passive
  performance indicators through integrations — project management tool activity
  (not content), communication frequency patterns, peer recognition indicators.

**Day 60 Lightweight Check-In**: A single-question pulse at Day 60 ("Still on
  track?") that supplements the Day 90 structured assessment.

**Learning Credits for Candidates**: Candidates who complete Day 30 and Day 90
  check-ins receive 50 Learning Credits each (contributing to CFS score improvement).

**Outcome Dispute Process**: Candidates who believe their Verified Performer assessment
  was inaccurate can file a structured dispute. A human review is conducted within
  14 days. Disputed outcomes are flagged as "Under Review" on the Prooffile during
  the review period.

**Privacy Controls**: All post-hire performance data is used only for RLWO training
  within the hiring employer's account. It is not shared with other employers, not
  visible on the candidate's public Prooffile as raw data, and is anonymized before
  any cross-employer training.

**Score Degradation Policy**: Dossyr scores have a 24-month active window. After
  24 months without retake activity, scores are marked as "Assessed [Date] — Consider
  Refreshing" with a visible retake prompt. This policy maintains score currency
  without penalizing candidates.

# SECTION 10: FAIRNESS AND COMPLIANCE ARCHITECTURE
================================================================================

## Four-Layer Framework

Fairness is not a feature added after the fact — it is built into the architecture
of every system that generates or uses candidate scores. Dossyr's Fairness and
Compliance Architecture operates across four layers with defined budgets, measurable
thresholds, and enforceable consequences.

---

### Layer 1 — Pre-Launch Audit ($15,000-$30,000)

Before any employer account activates and before any candidate assessment goes live,
Dossyr commissions an independent third-party bias audit of the ARISE and WSE scoring
frameworks.

**Audit Scope**:
- Statistical analysis of DMCS score distributions across demographic groups
  (race/ethnicity, gender, age band, national origin, educational background)
- Adverse impact analysis at standard 4/5ths rule threshold
- Review of LLM prompt templates for embedded cultural or linguistic bias
- Assessment of item difficulty calibration for culturally specific knowledge
- Evaluation of BQS and SA scoring rubrics for cross-cultural behavioral assumptions
- Review of scenario shell content for cultural and contextual universality

**Audit Standards**:
- EEOC Uniform Guidelines on Employee Selection Procedures (1978)
- Society for Industrial and Organizational Psychology (SIOP) Principles
- APA Standards for Educational and Psychological Testing
- EU AI Act requirements for high-risk AI systems in employment contexts

**Deliverable**: Signed third-party audit report with specific findings, required
  remediations, and recommended monitoring thresholds.

**Timeline**: Required before candidate 1 is assessed. Not negotiable.

---

### Layer 2 — Real-Time Bias Monitoring (Automated Dashboards)

Following launch, automated real-time monitoring tracks score distributions and
selection rates continuously across all candidate cohorts.

**Alert Thresholds** (trigger immediate review):

| Metric                                      | Alert Threshold                        |
|---------------------------------------------|----------------------------------------|
| DMCS score mean difference by gender        | > 0.3 standard deviations             |
| DMCS score mean difference by race/ethnicity| > 0.3 standard deviations             |
| Selection rate disparity (4/5ths rule)      | Selection rate < 80% of highest group |
| WSE completion rate disparity               | > 15% difference between cohorts      |
| CIQ flag rate disparity                     | > 2x flag rate between cohorts         |

When any alert threshold is triggered, the relevant scoring system is flagged for
human review. Automated alerts are sent to Dossyr's compliance team and the employer's
designated compliance contact. Assessment results for the affected cohort are placed
in "Under Review" status until cleared.

**Dashboard Visibility**: Employers with Professional+ subscriptions have direct
  access to real-time bias monitoring dashboards for their specific candidate pool.
  Enterprise subscribers receive quarterly bias review reports prepared by Dossyr's
  compliance team.

---

### Layer 3 — Structural Safeguards (Architectural, Not Policy)

The most reliable bias safeguards are those built into the system architecture —
not dependent on user compliance or policy guidance.

**Demographic-Blind Scoring Pipeline**: At no point in the ARISE or WSE scoring
  pipeline does the model receive, process, or weight any demographic information.
  Name, photo, age, gender, race, national origin, and educational institution are
  explicitly excluded from all scoring inputs.

**Feature Sanitization**: ARISE item content is filtered for culturally specific
  knowledge that would disadvantage candidates from specific cultural backgrounds.
  Items requiring country-specific regulatory knowledge, specific cultural idioms,
  or background only achievable through elite educational access are rejected.

**LLM Constraints**: All LLM interactions in the WSE operate under explicit prompt
  constraints prohibiting the generation of content that evaluates candidates based
  on cultural idiom familiarity, accent detection (voice moments), or response style
  patterns associated with specific demographic groups. Regular red-team testing
  validates constraint effectiveness.

**Multi-Standard Calibration**: ARISE item difficulty calibration is performed
  separately for multiple demographic cohorts to detect items with disproportionate
  difficulty for specific groups (differential item functioning analysis). Items with
  significant DIF are removed from production scoring.

**Non-Native Speaker Adjustment**: Candidates who self-identify as non-native English
  speakers activate an accommodation that adjusts CVS time benchmarks to account for
  documented response time differences in L2 language processing. This adjustment
  preserves speed measurement validity while removing linguistically-driven CVS
  disadvantage.

**ADA Accommodation Framework**: Candidates who self-identify as having a disability
  that affects assessment performance can request accommodations:
  - Extended time (1.5x or 2x)
  - Screen reader compatibility
  - Alternative response formats
  - Human-moderated voice moment replacement

  Accommodation requests are processed within 24 hours and do not require medical
  documentation for standard accommodations. Accommodation status does not affect
  Prooffile display or employer visibility.

---

### Layer 4 — Transparency and Accountability

**Published Methodology**: Dossyr publishes a plain-language methodology document
  describing how DMCS scores are calculated, what each dimension measures, and what
  the platform does and does not claim about predictive validity. This document is
  publicly accessible, not paywalled.

**Candidate Explanations**: Every candidate who completes an assessment receives a
  plain-language explanation of their scores — what was measured, how each dimension
  was calculated, and specifically what drove their score on each dimension.
  Candidates are never told "the AI decided." They receive substantive explanation.

**Employer Compliance Dashboard**: Enterprise employers have access to a full
  compliance dashboard including adverse impact analysis, audit logs of all
  assessment activity, and downloadable regulatory reports.

**Quarterly Red-Team Testing**: Dossyr conducts quarterly adversarial testing of
  all LLM-based scoring components to identify and remediate emergent bias patterns
  that may develop as models update.

**Annual Independent Audit**: An independent third-party audit of the full scoring
  framework is conducted annually. Results are shared with enterprise customers and
  published in summary form publicly.

**14-Day Appeals Process**: Candidates who believe their assessment was unfair or
  inaccurate can file a structured appeal. A human reviewer examines the session log
  and scoring audit trail within 14 days. Documented errors result in score
  recalculation. Undocumented concerns result in a written explanation.
